Understanding Exceptional Demand

Exceptional demand analysis highlights significant deviations in demand. The primary goal is to empower users with actionable insights by revealing where demand truly deviates from expected patterns, helping pinpoint real stock shortages versus other operational delays. Understanding these exceptional events is crucial for optimizing supply chain operations and maximizing customer satisfaction.

How Exceptional Demand is Detected

Exceptional demand is identified using a dynamic threshold for each product, based on data over the last 12 months. This threshold is calculated as: Product's Average Demand + (Multiplier × standarddeviation). The "Multiplier" increases with more historical data, making the threshold wider for well-established products. Items with 0 to 3 orderlines are always considered not exceptional. This ensures that only truly significant deviations are flagged as "exceptional," preventing false alerts.

The chart shows how the multiplier for standard deviation changes with historical orders, creating a more reliable baseline for detecting true exceptions.

Practical Example: How is Demand Determined?

Let's illustrate how a single demand event is categorized as exceptional or not, using a hypothetical product with 8 historical order lines.

Product A 100 units 10 units 2.20 122.0 units (100 + 2.20 * 10) 130 units EXCEPTIONAL

Aggregating the Results: The Big Picture

While individual lines are flagged, the overall impact is measured by aggregating these results. The total quantity from all exceptional order lines is summed and compared against the total requested quantity across all lines to determine the overall percentage of demand that was exceptional.

Key Data Filters & Analysis Scope

The accuracy of this analysis depends on strict data quality rules. The table below outlines the main criteria used to include or exclude transactions to ensure the analysis focuses on true external customer demand. To maintain this focus, certain orders like internal stock movements and special non-product types are also intentionally excluded.

Filter Category Description Action Reasoning
Requested Date Transactions from a specific date onwards (e.g., May 1, 2024) Included Defines the analysis period.
Exclude Setting Internal settings (e.g., standard orders, direct shipments, certain manual exclusions) Included Relevant demand types for this analysis.
Transaction Type Demo orders, returns, specific dropshipments, assemble-to-stock orders Excluded Not considered core customer demand.
Internal Sales (UD3) 'Internal Sales' (with specific system exceptions included) Excluded (with exception) Focuses on external demand.
Other UD3 Categories 'Purchase (Assembly)', 'Rest' Excluded Additional internal or non-demand related exclusions.
Call-off Orders Orders identified as 'call-off' types Excluded Not part of standard, repeatable demand.
Non-Stocked Items Items not managed in stock Excluded Analysis focuses on demand for stocked items.
